What earns a place here

  • Designed for external care, when care is needed
  • Minimal fragrance, minimal interference
  • Supports natural balance rather than “correcting” it
  • Clear ingredients and honest intent
  • Comfortable being one part of care — not the whole thing

What doesn’t

  • Products that promise control, “freshness,” or dominance
  • Anything meant to override natural balance
  • Internal “cleaning” framed as hygiene
  • Products that rely on irritation, numbing, or shock
  • Performance theater disguised as wellness

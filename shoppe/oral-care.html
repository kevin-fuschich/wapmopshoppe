Oral care

Mouth-safe picks that support comfort and taste like nothing. No burn. No “mint shock.” No performance theater.

  • No burn
  • Neutral taste
  • Comfort-first
Core pick

Plain, gentle mouth rinse

If you use rinse at all, choose something that doesn’t sting. Strong mint and alcohol are not “clean.” They’re sensations.

If it burns, don’t use it.

Read: Mouths as systems
Before

Soft toothbrush + boring toothpaste

Abrasion and harsh flavoring don’t help. A soft brush and a basic paste are usually better than “extreme” anything.

Comfort beats intensity.

Read: Mouths as systems
During

Hydration (yes, really)

Dry mouth is friction. Water is not a vibe-killer; it’s maintenance. Keep it nearby and treat it as normal.

Friction is information.

Read: Friction is information
After

Gentle cleanup + pause

Rinse, hydrate, and stop doing things for a bit. Aftercare is also about your mouth.

If soreness shows up: reduce pressure next time.

Read: After doesn’t mean absent

Oral care isn’t about making your mouth “strong.” It’s about keeping soft tissue comfortable and functional. If a product relies on shock (burn, sting, numb), it’s usually doing too much.

Should I worry about BPA in my toys?

Short answer: it’s reasonable to care about materials. It’s not necessary to panic.

  • Materials
  • External care
  • Boundaries

BPA (bisphenol A) is a chemical used in some plastics. It’s been studied for potential hormonal effects, especially with long-term exposure. That sounds dramatic — but context matters.

What actually matters

The biggest variables are:

  • Material type
  • Quality and manufacturing standards
  • Porosity
  • How the item is used and cleaned

Medical-grade silicone, stainless steel, and properly sealed glass are generally considered body-safe and non-porous. Cheap, unregulated plastics are less predictable.

This is about materials, not morality

There’s a lot of purity culture in “body-safe” marketing. The goal isn’t to be perfect. The goal is to reduce unnecessary exposure and friction.

If something smells strongly like chemicals, feels sticky even after washing, or degrades quickly, that’s information.

Comfort is the metric. If a material irritates you, it’s not the right one — regardless of marketing claims.

What to do

  • Choose reputable manufacturers.
  • Look for medical-grade silicone or non-porous materials.
  • Avoid mystery plastics with no listed material.
  • Replace items that degrade.

You don’t need to audit your entire life overnight. You just need to make boring, better choices over time.

Not medical advice. If you experience irritation or reactions, discontinue use and seek evaluation if needed.

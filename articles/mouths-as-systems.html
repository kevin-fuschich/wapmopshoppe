Mouths as systems

A mouth isn’t a tool. It’s a living system — temperature, moisture, pressure, and time. The best outcomes come from restraint and responsiveness.

The quiet rules

  • Moisture: dryness creates friction; friction creates irritation.
  • Pressure: consistent and gentle beats aggressive and inconsistent.
  • Time: rushed technique reads as performance, not care.

Before you start

Keep it simple. Avoid products that sting, tingle, numb, or “heat.” If it feels like a novelty, it often behaves like a novelty — unpredictable and irritating.

What to avoid

  • Alcohol-heavy mouthwash right before (drying/irritation).
  • Strong mint “burn” products (they don’t stay in the mouth).
  • Over-brushing or flossing hard right before (micro-irritation).
This isn’t a trick list. It’s systems etiquette.

Where “tongue in cheek” belongs

The tone can be playful. The behavior should be respectful. When someone trusts you with their body, you don’t improvise like it’s stand-up.

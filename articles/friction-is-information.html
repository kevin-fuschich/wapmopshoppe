Friction is information

Discomfort isn’t something to power through. Friction is information — and it’s usually telling the truth.

  • Friction
  • Comfort
  • Pace

A lot of people treat friction like a personal failure: not enough “prep,” not enough effort, not enough toughness. But friction is just physics meeting tissue. And tissue is allowed to have limits.

What friction is really pointing to

  • Pressure: too much force for the moment
  • Pace: too fast for the body to stay comfortable
  • Duration: too long without a reset
  • Lubrication: not enough moisture, or moisture in the wrong place
  • Timing: starting before bodies are ready

What helps (usually)

  • Slow down
  • Use less pressure
  • Take short resets instead of pushing through
  • Check in out loud — briefly, kindly, without making it a debate

What tends to make it worse

  • Trying to “push past” discomfort
  • Adding fragrance-y products to mask irritation
  • Assuming the body should “adapt” if you just keep going

The goal isn’t endurance. The goal is that bodies stay well together.

Related care

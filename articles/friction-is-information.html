Friction is information

Friction isn’t a challenge to overcome. It’s information about what the system needs.

Many routines treat friction like an obstacle: push through it, add pressure, add product, add determination. But friction usually means something is off.

What friction tells you

Dryness, resistance, discomfort — these are signals. They often mean there isn’t enough moisture, enough rest, or enough time for tissue to recover.

Ignoring those signals doesn’t make intimacy better. It just teaches the body to associate touch with stress.

When friction shows up, slow down. Add support — not force.

Sometimes that support is hydration. Sometimes it’s lubricant. Sometimes it’s stopping altogether. All three count as care.

We treat friction as information when selecting aftercare items .

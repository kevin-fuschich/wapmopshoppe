Dear Shoppe: we keep going until it hurts, then it’s awkward

Stopping early is not rejection. It’s respect. Here’s how to normalize comfort check-ins so you don’t end every moment with a wince.

  • Aftercare
  • Respect
  • Communication
  • Comfort

Dear Shoppe,
We tend to keep going until someone is uncomfortable, then it gets weird. Nobody wants to “ruin the mood,” but someone ends up sore. How do we fix this?

make stopping a normal skill

If the only “acceptable” end is finishing, people will push through discomfort. Swap the goal: comfort and closeness.

Try: “I’m into you. I want this to stay comfortable. Can we slow down / add lube / switch it up / pause for a reset?”

use a simple check-in question

  • “How’s your body?”
  • “Any friction?”
  • “More lube or slower?”

close the loop with aftercare

A rinse, water, towel, and calm presence turns “stopping” into “taking care.” That keeps closeness intact.

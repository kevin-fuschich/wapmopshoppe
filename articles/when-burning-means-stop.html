When burning means stop (a decision rule)

Burning isn’t “working.” Burning is a stop signal. Here’s a simple rule you can trust when your body is giving feedback.

  • Comfort
  • Friction
  • Skin
  • Aftercare

a rule you can use immediately

If a product causes sharp burning, stinging, or “cooling” that feels aggressive: stop using it. Rinse externally. Reset.

Comfort is not optional. If it burns, the system is not consenting.

what burning often means

  • skin barrier is irritated or disrupted
  • fragrance or additives are triggering sensitivity
  • friction has already done damage
  • too much washing or scrubbing

what to do instead

  • external-only, gentle wash
  • pat dry
  • reduce friction and heat
  • give it time

Dear Shoppe: my partner uses scented soap and it ruins everything

Fragrance is a frequent culprit — and a delicate conversation. Here’s how to talk about it without shame.

  • Respect
  • Communication
  • Skin
  • Comfort

Dear Shoppe,
My partner uses heavily scented soap and “deodorizing” body wash. After we’re close, I get irritated. I don’t want to embarrass them. But I also can’t keep doing this. What do I say?

name the effect, not the person

This is not “you smell.” This is “my skin reacts to fragrance.” Keep it physical and specific.

Try: “I’ve learned my skin is sensitive to fragrance. Can we switch to a simple unscented wash for intimate areas so it stays comfortable for both of us?”

make it collaborative

  • offer to buy a gentle wash together
  • frame it as comfort-first, not “cleaner”
  • suggest a reset ritual (wash + towel + water)

if they take it personally

Repeat the anchor: you’re not judging them — you’re protecting comfort. If comfort becomes negotiable, that’s a bigger conversation.

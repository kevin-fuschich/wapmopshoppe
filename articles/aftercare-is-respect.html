Aftercare is respect

Aftercare isn’t a genre. It’s how you close the loop — with attention, gentleness, and respect.

  • Aftercare
  • Respect
  • Comfort

A lot of people treat aftercare like a bonus — something you do if you’re “that kind of person,” or if the moment was intense. But aftercare is just maintenance: for bodies, for feelings, for trust.

Aftercare starts before anything starts

The simplest form of aftercare is clarity: what do you want, what do you not want, and how will you check in after? When people know they’ll be cared for on the other side, they relax into the middle.

What aftercare looks like (in the real world)

  • Water, warmth, a towel, a wipe — external-only, gentle cleanup
  • Lowering intensity: less pressure, fewer “extras,” more breathing room
  • Checking in: “How’s your body? Anything feel off?”
  • Respecting the answer without negotiation

What it’s not

  • Performative cuddling that ignores discomfort
  • A “fix” for going too hard
  • A script

Aftercare is how you prove the care was real — not just something you said on the way in.

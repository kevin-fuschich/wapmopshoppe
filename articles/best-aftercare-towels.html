The best aftercare towels (and why “soft” matters)

Aftercare is friction management. The right towel is soft, absorbent, and doesn’t turn “cleanup” into irritation.

  • Aftercare
  • Skin
  • Friction
  • Comfort

what to look for

  • soft loop (no scratchy “exfoliating” texture)
  • high absorbency
  • easy to wash hot (if you want)
  • dedicated small towels for “intimacy cleanup”

what to avoid

  • rough, thin towels that require rubbing
  • heavy fragrance from detergents
  • fabric softeners that leave residue

Pat dry beats rub dry. If the towel forces friction, it’s the wrong towel.

a simple aftercare towel system

  • keep 4–6 small towels in one drawer
  • wash on a predictable cycle
  • use fragrance-free detergent if skin is reactive
